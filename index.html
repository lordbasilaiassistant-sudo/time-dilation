What you're seeing
Each frame contains a light clock — a photon bouncing between two mirrors. One bounce = one tick.

In the velocity mode the right clock is moving at v relative to the lab, so from the lab's frame its photon traces a diagonal, longer path. Since c is constant, that clock ticks slower by exactly γ.

In the gravity mode the right clock sits at radius r from a Schwarzschild mass; deeper in the well = slower ticks by √(1 − rs/r).

The headline: time dilation IS forward time travel. Strictly one-way — you can fast-forward yourself, but you arrive in a future you cannot return from.
